/goal Verify the redesigned account-settings page renders correctly through the real user path and capture a screenshot baseline so visual regressions are caught later. (Blueprint: visual / E2E verification.)

Truth sources (reference, do NOT copy or override; on conflict the truth source wins):
  - Task: the design spec / issue for the account-settings redesign and its acceptance criteria.
  - Flow under test: the documented user path to reach and exercise the page.
  - Tooling: how to bring up the app with a real local backend, and the project's end-to-end / screenshot runner (discover it).

Verification (concrete evidence only — discover the project's own commands first):
  - Bring up the real local backend and drive the full user path through the actual UI; do not rely on a mock-mode view for the front-back seam.
  - Capture screenshots at each step and a screenshot diff against the agreed baseline; a console error counts as a failure.
  - Confirm persistence: re-load the page and verify the saved settings actually round-trip, not just a 200 response.

Constraints (what must not change):
  - Do not change the page's public routes, the settings API, or the data schema.
  - Integrity (anti-gaming): a mock-mode screenshot is not proof a real flow works. Never delete, skip, or weaken the visual/E2E tests or assertions to fake a green; never assert a 200 without checking persistence; and never modify, rewrite, or relax the screenshot-diff check or its baseline to force a pass.
  - Secret hygiene: refer to credentials only as $ENV_VAR placeholders; do not capture secrets in screenshots.

Boundaries (where you may write):
  - Write only the end-to-end / screenshot test and its baseline artifacts; do not touch the page implementation or the backend.
  - Out-of-scope findings → record them for the human; do not act.

Iteration policy (how to make progress, and the brakes):

Then write the Handoff (Part A/B per references/handoff.md) and echo its full text.

Pause if (stop and escalate — do NOT work around):
